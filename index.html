Every feature on our roadmap traces back to a specific time sink teachers described in interviews. ATP generation: hours lost writing lesson plans from scratch. Soal generation: converting formulas to images just to upload to Google Forms. AI grading: teachers avoiding essay questions entirely because there's no time to grade them. Jadwal: a week of manual scheduling "pakai perasaan." We didn't start with a feature list. We started with the workflow.

Attendance, gradebook, and rapor are table stakes. Everyone has them. And bolting AI onto an existing LMS won't fix this either — the AI needs structured curriculum data to work with, not just a gradebook.

Our Insight

Start where teachers start. Build from the curriculum, not the gradebook.

Pikira structures the Kurikulum Merdeka pipeline as connected data that AI can reason over. Because the curriculum is machine-readable, AI can generate ATPs, draft Modul Ajar, create soal, and grade against rubrics. None of which is possible when curriculum data lives in PDFs and Word docs.

CPCapaian Pembelajaran (learning outcomes)→ TPTujuan Pembelajaran (learning objectives)→ ATPAlur Tujuan Pembelajaran (learning objective sequence)→ Modul Ajar→ Assessment→ Grade→ Rapor

Grounded in Kurikulum Merdeka

Every AI generation draws from structured CP/TP data and public BSKAPBadan Standar, Kurikulum, dan Asesmen Pendidikan PDFs, not generic knowledge. ATPs reference real learning objectives.

School-Specific Context

As teachers use Pikira, it learns their context: their ATP, their assessments, their grading patterns. Over time, AI suggestions get more specific to their school. This is where we're building toward.

Gets smarter with use

Every assessment graded feeds back into smarter suggestions. The more a school uses Pikira, the better it gets. Generic AI can't compete.

🤖 Why not just ChatGPT? → Context is the moat. It doesn't know your curriculum, students, or school.

↓ Still skeptical? We answered 12 objections below.

Questions you're probably already thinking.

We'd rather answer them here than dodge them in the meeting.

"You're 2 people pre-launch. Why should I bet my time on this?"

Pre-launch, yes. Pre-work, no. In 4 weeks we talked to 2 kepala sekolah and 10 teachers, registered a PT Perorangan, built 2 working AI features (ATP generation, Modul Ajar generation), and hired a designer with UI in active development. We have 2 schools in active conversation. Our plan is to formalize design partnerships in April: teachers testing the product and shaping it with us. Paid pilots follow once the product is proven. Then we hit the July-August adoption window when schools reset for the new academic year. We know what we're doing next.

"The EdTech graveyard is full of LMS startups."

Existing players (Sokrates, Quintal, Pijar) digitize the output: attendance, grades, rapor. Pikira digitizes the workflow that produces them: curriculum planning, assessment creation, grading. That's not a feature gap you can close with a plugin. It's a foundation difference. Same reason Linear couldn't be a Jira plugin.

"You don't have product-market fit yet."

We don't, and we're not pretending we do. What we have is strong problem validation: every school described the same pain: hours lost on planning and grading, multiple tools for a single workflow, data re-entered across systems. Teachers are already using ChatGPT to cope, which tells you demand exists but the solution doesn't. Next steps: design partnerships in April (teachers testing and shaping the product), iterate through the semester with real usage data, then convert to paid pilots once the product is proven. Proof points ready for the July-August tool adoption window.

"AI features are easy to copy. What's the moat?"

Two layers. First, architectural: our curriculum pipeline is structured data that AI can reason over. Competitors would need to rebuild from scratch. Second, context. Pikira pulls from public textbook PDFs (SIBISistem Informasi Buku Indonesia/Buku Sekolah Elektronik), the teacher's own ATP, their RPP, their past interactions. ChatGPT generates generic lesson plans. Pikira generates contextual ones, aligned to the specific kurikulum, the specific school's ATP, the specific teacher's style. That context compounds with every use, and you can't replicate it without the same integrated data layer.

"What happens if P2MW doesn't work out?"

P2MW is a forcing function, not a dependency. We chose it over UI Incubate specifically because the 70/30 IP royalty split was unacceptable. We want connections and structure, not money. The Rp 15M funding is nice but not critical. If P2MW falls through, the company continues. We have a registered PT, a working product, and a pilot pipeline.

"How do you actually make money? Schools are slow to pay."

Per-school SaaS subscription, tiered: Rp 2M/month for negeri and small schools, Rp 4.5M mid-tier private, Rp 5.5M large/elite private. We start with private schools because they have budget authority and faster decision cycles. Conservative target: 10 schools at Rp 4.5M average = Rp 45M MRR by end of 2026. The buyer is the kepala sekolah, GTM is warm intros through teacher networks.

"Who's the buyer and who's the user?"

The user is the teacher. The buyer is the kepala sekolah. Our play: land through teacher adoption (they feel the pain), then the kepala sekolah sees consolidated data and reduced admin burden. We don't sell top-down first. We get teachers using it, then convert the school.

"You have a lot of features listed. What's the prioritization?"

Ship what teachers need to survive the pilot: soal generation, AI grading, jadwal algorithm. Defer everything else. School ops expansion (SPPSumbangan Pembinaan Pendidikan (tuition billing), PPDBPenerimaan Peserta Didik Baru (student admissions), parent comms) is explicitly post-traction. This is actually exactly why we want you. We know we'll face hard tradeoffs once pilots start, and we need someone who's done this before to help us figure out what to cut.

"What was the negative signal from validation?"

Adoption resistance at scale. Pijar was deployed at one school but only 12 of ~70 teachers actually used it. Teachers default to what they know: Excel, Word, WhatsApp. Our bet is that covering enough of their workflow makes switching cost worth it. But we haven't proven that yet. That's what pilots are for.

"What's your technical risk?"

AI quality on Indonesian curriculum content. ATP and Modul Ajar generation work well, but essay grading in Bahasa Indonesia with rubric alignment is genuinely hard. If AI grading isn't trustworthy, teachers won't use it, and that's a big part of our value prop. We're mitigating by grounding AI with contextual data: SIBISistem Informasi Buku Indonesia textbooks, the teacher's own materials, their rubric definitions.

"What does success look like in 6 months? What kills you?"

Success: 5-10 schools on paid pilots, teachers using it daily, 2-3 referral intros from kepala sekolah. What kills us: teachers try it and churn because AI output quality isn't good enough, or onboarding friction is too high and schools stall after signing up.

"Why me specifically?"

Because we don't need a signature. Your PPD class taught us how to think about product development. We want to apply that thinking to a real product, with you pushing back on our decisions. You know what kills startups at this stage. Not the obvious stuff, the subtle stuff. Wrong sequencing, building for the wrong persona, over-engineering before validation.
