The Problem

The academic operation has no system underneath it.

We talked to 2 principals and 10 teachers across 2 schools. They all described the same thing.

The Teacher's Day

  • 10+ disconnected tools: Excel, Word, Forms, e-RaporGovernment report card system, ChatGPT, DapodikData Pokok Pendidikan (national education database system)
  • Grading is the biggest time sink - and results are flat numbers. A student gets 60, but nobody knows which learning objectives they're struggling with.
  • 70% of teachers still building basic IT fluency

"Guru capeknya di administrasi."

The Principal's Blindspot

  • Teaching quality is invisible to leadership. Monitoring = CCTV and hallway walks.
  • Data exists in gradebooks and LMS dashboards, but it's per-assessment averages - nobody knows which learning objectives are the problem.
  • Problems surface at rapat plenoEnd-of-semester plenary meeting for grade finalization, when it's too late. When yayasanSchool foundation (governing body) asks why Rapor PendidikanSchool-level performance report from Kemendikbud scores are flat, there's no structured answer.

The School's Survival

  • Private schools face declining enrollment from zonasiGovernment zoning policy that assigns students to nearest public school, increasing competition for private schools, birth rates, and competition
  • Reputation depends on demonstrable quality
  • No tool helps schools run academic work as one integrated system

Six steps, four tools, one exam

SoalExam/test questions in Word, formulas to images, upload to Forms, grade in Excel, copy into e-RaporGovernment report card system. "She wants to input once and have it flow everywhere."

Tools that fight the teacher

SokratesCompetitor: school management system for private schools blocks late submissions entirely. Teachers collect late work on WhatsApp. When the tool doesn't match the workflow, teachers route around it.

~50% of planning already uses AI, but output breaks between tools. The demand is proven. The solution doesn't exist.

The Pattern

AI-native already won in every other vertical.

The pattern is always the same: the winner doesn't improve the old tool. They change who does the work.

Ramp

AI handles expense categorization and close - finance teams review. CFOs see company-wide spending patterns in real time. Valued at $32B.

Harvey

AI drafts contracts and does document review - lawyers focus on judgment. Partners see firm-wide productivity patterns for the first time. Valued at $11B.

Numeric

AI prepares reconciliations and close checklists - accountants review. Controllers see close progress in real time. Raised $28M Series A.

In each case, the winner built from scratch. The incumbents with decades of distribution and resources couldn't retrofit.

No one has done this for schools yet.

Questions you're probably already thinking.

We'd rather answer them here than dodge them in the meeting.

"You're 2 people pre-launch. Why should I bet my time on this?"

Pre-launch, yes. Pre-work, no. In 4 weeks: 12 interviews, 2 working AI features, a hired designer, and 2 schools in active conversation. Design partnerships start April, iterate through the semester, hit the July-August adoption wave. We know what we're doing next.

"The EdTech graveyard is full of LMS startups."

SokratesCompetitor: school management system for private schools, QuintalCompetitor: school admin & academic platform, PijarCompetitor: Kemendikbud-backed LMS, free for public schools digitize documents - attendance, grades, raporStudent report card. Pikira digitizes the workflow that produces them. Their data models assume teachers do the work and software stores it. To generate curriculum-aligned content and feed results back into planning, you'd redesign from the ground up.

"AI features are easy to copy. What's the moat?"

Two layers. Architecture: our curriculum pipeline is structured data AI can reason over - competitors would rebuild from scratch. Context: Pikira generates from the teacher's own ATPAlur Tujuan Pembelajaran, Modul AjarTeaching module, and past results. After one semester, it knows your school's specific patterns - which learning objectives are weak, which classes diverge, which students need attention. A competitor copying features tomorrow doesn't get that data.

"What was the negative signal from validation?"

Adoption resistance at scale. PijarKemendikbud-backed LMS was deployed at one school but only 12 of ~70 teachers actually used it. Teachers default to what they know: Excel, Word, WhatsApp. Our bet is that covering enough of their workflow makes switching cost worth it. But we haven't proven that yet. That's what pilots are for.

"What does success look like in 6 months? What kills you?"

Success: 3-5 schools on pilots, teachers using it daily, 2-3 referral intros from principals. What kills us: AI output isn't good enough and teachers churn, or onboarding friction stalls adoption. Teachers already cope with PMMPlatform Merdeka Mengajar: free government platform for teachers, ChatGPT, and reused files - Pikira has to feel meaningfully better in week one, or they revert. Speed to the feedback loop - where school data makes the copilot smarter - is the moat against incumbents with more distribution.
